Get to Know Me

I have been an Arabic TV Presenter and producer for the last decade. Over this time, I have developed a determination and confidence that has allowed my work to be of the highest quality, no matter the pressures or deadlines that are required. In 2009, I was awarded a Middle Eastern Oscar for being the most popular and youngest TV presenter in the Middle East. That moment set the stage for me to consistently be the best presenter and producer that I can be.

Words like 'glamorous' are thrown around quite a bit when it comes to the entertainment business from those that are not directly involved in it. I totally see why that is. It all looks like one champagne-filled after-party, but in reality, it is a business full of rejection and judgment, that can fill even the most confident and thick-skinned soul with self-doubt and feelings of insecurity. What matters is to understand that it's two sides of the same coin. To have the opportunity to create content in whatever capacity it may be, means that opinions, brutally honest feedback, and a series of flat out 'NO's' are going to be par for the course. But, it is these tough moments that make us better. They make us better producers and writers, better presenters and camera people, better at communicating, better at understanding, better at life in general.

a wonderful journey...

As a TV Presenter, it is my job to relay information to the audience in a way that is entertaining, engaging, and also informative. When I conduct an interview, not only do I make sure the person I am speaking to feels comfortable and at ease, I also assist them in providing the best answers possible, so that the final segment can do the topic of choice total justice. This also applies to my work as a red carpet presenter, where I have reported at various movie premieres and award shows, including Twilight, the Hunger Games, Inside Out, and The Jungle Book.

A highlight of mine was covering the Pre-Oscar festivities where legendary chef, Wolfgang Puck, presented to the media what the celebrities would be eating on the night of the big show. Around that same time, I was invited to interview the Master Interviewer himself, Larry King, making me the first and only Arabic TV presenter to interview him one on one.

I am no stranger to professional and personal challenges. Coming from another country and having to master the English language hasn't been an easy road, especially when most of my job involves asking questions, but it has provided me such gratitude that I even have the chance to pursue this line of work and live my dream. When I first arrived to the States, I could barely get access to a movie premiere no matter how hard I tried, but now, years later, I have done more then I could have ever dreamed of. I never thought I would find myself sitting next to Bradley Cooper's table at the White House Corespondent Dinner, but that's how this business works. What once seemed like nothing more than a daydream, one day becomes reality.

I have never once viewed my time as a presenter/producer as “work, or as my “job.” It is merely an extension of who I am. I have always been fascinated by people and the stories they have to tell. It is through asking the right questions we learn others' dreams, goals, hopes, fears, struggles, and passions. This is what being a TV presenter is all about. We help others tell their story in a way that can help connect them to an audience on a deep and emotional level.
